Back-to-School: Modern Sciences Bridge

Inquiry-driven Science with algorithmic thinking, mapped to Cambridge Lower Secondary Science & Global Perspectives. EAL-friendly through CLIL routines. Run as a 6–8 week starter strand.

Mapped to: Thinking & Working Scientifically • Science in Context Safeguarding-aware • GDPR-compliant

Clear outcomes in 6–8 weeks

We target enquiry skills and language functions. Evidence is collected weekly.

Thinking & Working Scientifically

Plan, test, measure, explain. From hypotheses to conclusions with uncertainty awareness.

Data & Algorithms

Block diagrams, if/else, variables. Simple sensors or virtual labs to gather and reason with data.

Academic English

CLIL scaffolding: describe → explain → compare → conclude. CEFR-aware prompts and sentence frames.

Built around Cambridge Lower Secondary

We use Thinking & Working Scientifically as the backbone, draw contexts from Biology, Chemistry, Physics, and finish with a Global Perspectives mini-inquiry.

What this means in class

  • CLIL staging: input → guided output → free output.
  • Dual targets: science objective + language function.
  • Artefacts: lab notes, diagrams, short videos, viva.

Safeguarding & operations

  • KCSIE-aware delivery; GDPR-compliant data flow.
  • Hybrid or on-site. 60–75 min sessions. Groups ≤ 16.
  • Resourcing: micro:bit/virtual labs; printable booklets.

What we teach (example modules)

A • Enquiry × Data

Hypothesis → test → communicate. Mini-investigations with simple datasets and visualisations.

B • Algorithms × Measurement

if/else, variables, loops. Sensor-based or virtual experiments. Error and uncertainty.

C • Science in Context

Local issue capstone (e.g., air quality). Team poster + 3-min viva following GP routines.

Evidence & reporting

We share progress artefacts and concise reports. No extra exam burden.

Weekly artefacts

Photos/scans of lab notes, diagrams, short clips. Rubric snapshots aligned to enquiry & communication.

End-of-pilot report

Cohort summary, sample work, attendance, recommendations for rollout.

FAQ

Is this endorsed by Cambridge?

No. It’s independently developed and aligned to public framework statements.

Do we need devices?

Not strictly. We can run with printable booklets and lightweight demos; devices enable richer data tasks.

How is safeguarding handled?

Delivery is KCSIE-aware; artefact handling follows GDPR. Media capture is opt-in and configurable.
