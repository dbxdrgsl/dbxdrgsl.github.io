/spain – A Different Kind of Warmth

“If the Netherlands is where I work, Spain might be where I breathe.”

My Thoughts on Spain

I don’t speak Spanish (yet), and I’m not in love with heat — but there’s something about Spain that pulls at me in a different register. A feeling of rhythm. Of time that flows slower, but deeper.

What I Admire

  • The strong public culture: plazas, late dinners, city life that's alive well into the night
  • The regional depth — Catalonia, Basque Country, Andalusia — all layered, complex, and full of story
  • The emphasis on living well: food, friendship, and taking your time
  • The architecture and mathematics in motion — from Gaudí to Alhambra geometry

Spain as a Second Option?

If the Netherlands is my structured future, Spain is my poetic one. I don’t know if I’ll move there, but I know I want to understand it. Visit longer. Think slower. Maybe write there.

Possibilities

  • Summer teaching programs, Erasmus+ collaborations, digital residencies
  • Studying regional languages as a side project — Catalan or Basque, even
  • Exploring university partnerships for ICT and educational technology projects

Closing Note

I don’t romanticize Spain as an escape. I see it as a calibration — a chance to find out what pace actually suits me. Somewhere between the flamenco rhythms and philosophical siestas, there might be a version of me that feels more human.
